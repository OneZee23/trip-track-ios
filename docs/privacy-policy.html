Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 20, 2026

1. Overview

TripTrack is an iOS application for recording personal car trips. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. It applies to the TripTrack mobile app and any server infrastructure we operate ("Service").

The developer of TripTrack ("we", "us") acts as the data controller for personal data processed through the Service. Contact: privacy@trip-track.app.

2. Two Usage Modes

TripTrack works in two distinct modes. The data we process depends on which mode you use.

2.1 Guest mode (offline, default)

If you do not sign in, TripTrack stores all your data — trips, GPS tracks, photos, vehicle profiles, preferences — exclusively on your device using Apple's CoreData framework. No data leaves the device. In this mode we do not process any personal data on our servers.

2.2 Signed-in mode (cloud sync)

If you sign in with Apple, your data can be synchronized with our server so it is available on your other devices. Cloud sync is opt-in and can be disabled at any time in the app (Profile → Cloud Sync).

3. Data We Process

3.1 Account data (signed-in mode only)

  • Apple user identifier — an opaque stable ID provided by Apple after Sign in with Apple. We never receive your Apple ID or password.
  • Email — provided by Apple on first sign-in (real or hide-my-email alias). Optional.
  • Display name — provided by Apple on first sign-in. Optional.
  • Device identifier — a random UUID generated locally on first app launch, used to tell your devices apart.

3.2 Trip and vehicle data (signed-in mode only, when cloud sync is on)

  • GPS trajectories — coordinates, speed, altitude, and timestamps captured during trip recording you initiated.
  • Trip metadata — start/end time, distance, duration, auto-geocoded place names, user-provided titles and notes.
  • Photos you attach — uploaded to Cloudflare R2 storage. Before upload, all metadata (EXIF, including GPS tags) is stripped.
  • Vehicle profiles — make, model, avatar, odometer.
  • App settings — theme, language, unit system, gamification progress.

3.3 Technical data

  • Authentication tokens — JWT access and refresh tokens stored in your iOS Keychain.
  • IP address — seen by our server on each request; kept in access logs for up to 14 days for abuse prevention, then discarded.

3.4 Local-only data (never leaves your device)

  • Recent 48 hours of diagnostic logs (you can export them via Profile → Send debug logs).
  • Geocode cache, map tiles, fog-of-war state.

4. Location Data — Detail

  • Location is accessed only during active trip recording that you start manually, or when you opt in to automatic recording via Bluetooth/motion triggers.
  • Background location permission is used solely to continue an in-progress recording.
  • You can revoke location access at any time in iOS Settings.
  • Reverse geocoding (coordinates → place names) is performed via Apple MapKit on your device. We do not use third-party geocoding services.

6. Sub-processors

When you use cloud sync, the following parties process data on our behalf or alongside us:

  • Apple Inc. (United States) — Sign in with Apple identity-token verification. Apple is an independent controller of your Apple ID; we only receive a limited identity token. Apple is certified under the EU-US Data Privacy Framework.
  • Cloudflare, Inc. (United States / EU data centers) — photo object storage (Cloudflare R2), in an EU-jurisdiction bucket. Cloudflare is certified under the EU-US Data Privacy Framework and our processing is governed by Cloudflare's Data Processing Addendum incorporating EU Standard Contractual Clauses (2021/914).
  • Our hosting provider — runs the application server and PostgreSQL database. Currently an EU-based VPS provider; the exact name will be published here before the first public release. Data center in the European Economic Area. Governed by the provider's GDPR-compliant DPA.

We do not sell or share your personal information. We do not use analytics SDKs, advertising networks, cross-app/site tracking, or any third-party trackers. We do not share data with advertisers. We do not use your content to train machine-learning models.

7. International Data Transfers

Our primary hosting is in the European Economic Area. Photo storage on Cloudflare R2 uses an EU-jurisdiction bucket; however, as a US-headquartered company, Cloudflare is subject to US law. We rely on Cloudflare's EU-US Data Privacy Framework certification and Standard Contractual Clauses as transfer mechanisms. Sign in with Apple authentication routes through Apple Inc. (United States) under the EU-US DPF. If you are in a jurisdiction with stricter cross-border rules, using cloud sync constitutes your informed consent to this transfer.

8. Data Retention

  • Account data and synced content — retained for as long as your account is active. If you don't sign in for 3 years, we may delete the account after notifying you via the email on file.
  • Deleted trips, photos, vehicles, and account data — removed from our primary database and from Cloudflare R2 immediately upon deletion.
  • Encrypted database backups — retained for no more than 30 days. Deleted user data is purged from backups within this window by backup rotation.
  • Server access logs — up to 14 days, then deleted.
  • Account-deletion audit record — a non-identifying record (timestamp + opaque hash, no name, email, or content) may be retained indefinitely to demonstrate compliance with deletion requests.
  • Abuse reports and moderation actions — retained for up to 2 years for safety and enforcement, then deleted.
  • Authentication refresh tokens — rotated on use, revoked on sign-out or account deletion.

9. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you have the following rights:

  • Access and portability — request a copy of your data. Contact us at privacy@trip-track.app.
  • Rectification — correct inaccurate data (most fields are editable directly in the app).
  • Erasure — permanently delete your account and all associated server-side data via Profile → Cloud Sync → Delete account. Local trips on your device are preserved after account deletion; you can continue using the app in guest mode.
  • Portability — receive a machine-readable copy of your data. Email privacy@trip-track.app and we will deliver a JSON export (with photos as a ZIP) within 30 days.
  • Restriction and objection — limit or stop certain processing. Disable cloud sync in the app at any time.
  • Withdraw consent — sign out at any time; cloud processing stops immediately.
  • Complain — lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

We respond to verified requests within 30 days.

10. Security

  • All network communication is over HTTPS.
  • Authentication credentials are stored in iOS Keychain (hardware-backed on most devices).
  • Passwords are never used — authentication is via Sign in with Apple only.
  • Photos are stripped of metadata (EXIF including GPS) before upload.
  • Database access is restricted to the application service.

No system is perfectly secure. If you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately.

11. Children's Privacy

TripTrack is directed to adults and is not intended for children. The minimum age to use the App is 16 (matching the strictest GDPR Art. 8 threshold across EU member states). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13 in the United States (COPPA), and will delete any account we learn belongs to a user under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us for prompt removal.

11a. US State-Specific Disclosures

California (CCPA/CPRA). We do not sell or share your personal information as defined by California law. We do not use personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. If you are a California resident, you may contact us to exercise rights to know, delete, correct, or access your data. We will verify your identity via the email on file.

Nevada (SB 220). Nevada residents may opt out of any future sale of their covered personal information by emailing privacy@trip-track.app. We currently do not sell personal information.

Washington (My Health My Data Act). Precise location data collected by the App may, in some cases, incidentally reveal visits to health-related facilities (e.g., a medical office, gym). To the extent any such data is classified as "consumer health data" under Washington law: (a) we collect it only when you actively record a trip; (b) we do not sell it; (c) we do not share it with advertisers; (d) we do not geofence health-facility locations; (e) you can delete it at any time by deleting the relevant trip or your account. Before any material change to this practice, we will obtain your separate opt-in consent. To exercise rights under RCW 19.373, email privacy@trip-track.app.

Other US states (Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Texas, Oregon, Montana, etc.). Residents of states with comprehensive privacy laws have rights similar to California's under their respective statutes. To exercise any such right, email privacy@trip-track.app.
